# Starfall Chronicles — Story Bible

This is the single source of truth for the narrative of Starfall Chronicles. Future agents adding chapters (Acts 2, 3, and beyond) must keep this document current and treat it as canon. When the narrative in code (`src/data/dialogue/`, `src/data/story/`) conflicts with this document, trust the data files and update the bible.

## 1. World Overview

The year is **1157 Post-Collapse**. Four hundred years ago, on a night now preserved only in fragmented songs and corrupted archives, the stars began to go out. The event — **the Collapse** — shattered the interplanetary civilization of the Algol system. Starships fell silent mid-void. Colonies on every world went dark within a single generation. What remains is a scatter of survivor settlements clinging to the bones of a lost age.

The story opens on **Dezolis**, the outermost habitable world of Algol: a dying ice planet of permafrost valleys, frozen seas, and ruined orbital facilities half-buried in snow. Its sun is failing. Its people are few.

The cause of the Collapse — and the force still extinguishing the stars one by one — is **the Bane**: a cosmic darkness, vast and alien, whose generals (Shadowwardens) command hosts of corrupted beasts. The Bane is not merely an enemy. It is an appetite, older than Algol itself.

Hope survives in one place: the **Stellar Archive**, a pre-Collapse data vault hidden deep within the ruins of **Motavia**. Legend holds it contains a weapon capable of ending the Bane. The coordinates were lost with the Collapse — until now.

The setting blends swords and photon blades, faith and physics. Practitioners channel **techniques** (never called "magic") — disciplined manipulation of photonic energy. **CASTs** — self-aware android frames from the old world — walk alongside organics. Interplanetary travel via void drives is the stuff of legend, not industry.

## 2. Characters

### Kael — Hunter (male)
A Dezolis-born Hunter who has spent his adult life scavenging crash wrecks and escorting settlers through Shadowbeast country. Pragmatic, soft-spoken, and quietly courageous. He has buried more friends than he admits. Kael believes in small, doable things: finish the next ridge, protect the next village, get the next person home alive. He resents grand prophecies on principle, which makes his role in one deeply inconvenient. Carries a photon blade handed down from his father.

### Lyra — Esper (female)
An idealistic young Esper trained by one of the last surviving shrine-orders of Dezolis. She is attuned to techniques at a level few still living can match, and senses the Bane's corruption as a pressure against her mind. She lost her family and her teacher in the slow dying of her home village, and carries that grief as fuel rather than weight. Lyra believes the old world can be remembered into something new; she is the voice that pushes the party forward when exhaustion tells Kael to stop.

### ARIEL — CAST (android)
Unit designation **ARIEL-7**. A pre-Collapse CAST, dormant in a stasis pod at the crash site for 412 years. She carries encrypted coordinates to the Stellar Archive — the mission she was launched to complete on the night the stars began to fall. ARIEL speaks with precise, occasionally archaic cadence, using terminology and protocol references no longer meaningful to anyone alive. She is adjusting, in real time, to a world 400 years changed from the one she was built to serve. Her certainty wavers; her mission does not.

### The Shadowwarden — Act 1 Boss
A general of the Bane's host, glimpsed in Act 1 as the guardian of the Dezolis Spaceport. Speaks in the voice of one who has waited centuries for the sound of footsteps. Its defeat is a victory and a warning: Shadowwardens are not singular. There are more. A hierarchy stretches above them into darker places.

### The Bane
The cosmic antagonist. Not a god, not a demon, not fully revealed in Act 1. An ancient, alien hunger that eats stars and hollows worlds. Its motives, origin, and connection to the Collapse will be revealed in Act 3. For now it is a shadow on the edge of every scene, patient and vast. It does not forget small defiances.

## 3. Act Summaries

### Act 1 — Echoes of the Fallen Star *(implemented — Demo v0.3)*
On Dezolis, a falling star brings Kael and Lyra to a centuries-old crash site outside Crestfall Village. Inside a still-active stasis pod they find ARIEL, who carries the coordinates to the Stellar Archive. The party fights through Shadowbeasts at the crash site, crosses the corrupted Dezolis Wilds, and reaches an abandoned pre-Collapse spaceport. There they defeat a Shadowwarden guarding the hangar and bring a long-dormant **Landale-class courier ship** online. Act 1 closes with Dezolis falling away below as the party sets course for Motavia — and the Bane taking notice.

### Act 2 — The Void Between Worlds *(planned)*
The party's maiden voyage across the interstellar dark between Dezolis and Motavia. Locations: the Landale's corridors, **The Void** itself (with its own hazards), and the **Motavia Desert** on arrival. On Motavia they find that the Bane has already reached ahead of them — outposts are falling, and Shadowbeasts walk in daylight. The party allies with **Motavian survivors** (native desert-dwellers, proud and wary of off-worlders) to locate the buried entrance to the Stellar Archive. Ends with the entrance opened — and an ambush.

### Act 3 — Light of the Archive *(planned)*
The party descends into the **Stellar Archive** beneath the Motavian ruins. The truth is revealed: **the Collapse was not a natural disaster**. The Bane engineered it, four centuries ago, to blind and weaken Algol before its final harvest. The Archive contains a weapon — but activating it requires a sacrifice no one is prepared to pay. The climax forces a choice that defines the characters and the fate of the system. The final confrontation in the **Bane Rift** ends the first arc of the Chronicles.

## 4. Tone and Voice Guide

Starfall Chronicles is **grounded**, not operatic. Characters are tired but not broken; hopeful but not naive. Villains are patient and certain, not cackling. Dialogue should sound like something a real person under pressure would actually say.

**Hope vs. despair.** The world is dying, but the party is not mourning — they are working. Levity breaks through in small moments: a dry line between two exhausted allies, a quiet marvel at something old and still beautiful. Never cheerful. Never nihilistic.

**Voices:**
- **Kael** — Short sentences. Direct verbs. Dry humor in dark moments ("We're deep in Shadowbeast territory. Whatever's in there, we wake it fast or not at all."). Avoids abstractions. Calls a thing what it is.
- **Lyra** — More reflective. References to what was before, to the land's sickness, to what she can sense that others can't. Speaks in full sentences; sometimes in images. Her grief is present but never performed.
- **ARIEL** — Precise, slightly archaic. Uses pre-Collapse designations and protocol references that land as curiosities on modern ears ("pre-Collapse Dezolis Orbital Facility Seven"). Adapting; occasionally asks what a word still means.

**Rules of thumb.** No exposition dumps — world-building emerges through character reactions to what they see. Earn emotional beats; don't announce them. Characters can face real loss. A sacrifice should cost the party something they cannot get back. Phantasy Star tradition: the small and the cosmic coexist in the same scene.

## 5. Lore Glossary

- **Post-Collapse calendar** — Years counted from the Collapse. The demo opens in 1157 PC; the Collapse occurred ~400 years ago (so Year 0 PC ≈ real 757 before the demo).
- **The Collapse** — The cataclysm ~400 years ago that ended interplanetary civilization in Algol. Cause: the Bane (revealed in Act 3).
- **Dezolis** — Dying ice planet on the outer rim of the Algol system; setting of Act 1. Temperatures reach -42°C in the Wilds.
- **Motavia** — Desert world; setting of Acts 2 and 3. Home of the Motavian survivors and the Stellar Archive.
- **Algol system** — The star system containing Dezolis, Motavia, and other (lost) worlds once linked by void drive.
- **The Bane** — The cosmic darkness devouring the stars; the primary antagonist force of the entire arc.
- **Shadowwardens** — Generals of the Bane's host; the Act 1 boss is one. A hierarchy exists above them.
- **Shadowbeasts** — Corrupted creatures serving the Bane. Variants include Shadowpups, Shadowhounds, Ice Crawlers, and Void Stalkers.
- **Techniques** — Disciplined manipulation of photonic energy; the setting's "magic." Never called magic. Examples: FOI (fire), RES (heal), RAY (light).
- **CASTs** — Pre-Collapse self-aware android units. ARIEL is a CAST. Extremely rare in the current era.
- **The Landale** — Pre-Collapse Landale-class courier ship recovered in Act 1; the party's vessel for Act 2 onward.
- **The Stellar Archive** — Pre-Collapse data vault on Motavia; holds the weapon to destroy the Bane and the truth of the Collapse.
- **The Void** — The interstellar dark between Algol's worlds; traversed by void drive. Has its own hazards.
- **Meseta** — The currency of Algol, still honored among surviving settlements.
